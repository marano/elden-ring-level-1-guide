First, the rules of engagement

The Doctrine

At Rune Level 1 your stats are frozen at 10 across the board. You will never out-damage or out-tank anything by leveling — so the entire run is built on damage sources that ignore your level. Internalize these six pillars and the rest is just knowing where to find the pieces.

PILLAR I

Status > stats

Bleed, rot, frost and sleep deal damage as a % of the target's health or as flat ticks — none of it scales with your level. This is your main gun.

PILLAR II

Upgrade, don't level

Smithing Stones raise a weapon's base damage regardless of your stats. Every rune goes to upgrades, flasks, stones and consumables — never to a level.

PILLAR III

The Soreseal crutch

Radagon's Soreseal grants +5 Vigor, Endurance, Str & Dex. On a Wretch that's ~26% more HP and enough stats to actually wield your key weapons.

PILLAR IV

Turtle & roll

A 100%-physical-block shield plus an evasion Ash of War (Quickstep → Bloodhound's Step) means most hits cost stamina, not your tiny health bar.

PILLAR V

Let others fight

Spirit Ashes tank aggro and stack their own status. Upgraded with Glovewort, their power is independent of your level entirely.

PILLAR VI

Scadutree (DLC)

In Shadow of the Erdtree, Scadutree Fragments raise damage & defense with zero regard for level. They are your only "leveling" in the DLC — hoard them.

The single most important rule

A weapon you don't meet the stat requirements for still applies its full status buildup — only the raw physical/elemental damage is penalized. That's why a Rivers of Blood or an Antspur Rapier is devastating at RL1: the bleed and rot procs land at full strength no matter how weak your Arcane or Dexterity is. Two-handing also multiplies your Strength by 1.5× (so 10 Str counts as 15) for meeting requirements.

Rune economy. Farm just enough runes for: flask upgrades (Golden Seeds/Sacred Tears are found, not bought), Smithing Stone Bell Bearings' stock, greases and pots, and a spirit ash or two. Because you never level, runes pile up fast — hold them and dump into upgrades whenever you unlock a new stone tier.

Defense & utility kit

  • A 100%-physical-block shield with a low Str requirement — e.g. the Beast Crest Heater Shield (Str 8). Block-and-poke is your safety net against physical bosses.
  • Quickstep → Bloodhound's Step — evasion Ashes of War with generous i-frames. Bloodhound's Step is the single best defensive tool in the game.
  • Seppuku (Ash of War) — self-inflicts bleed to massively boost your weapon's bleed damage. Use carefully with your small health bar; re-apply from a safe distance.
  • Greases — Blood Grease / Rot Grease let any weapon carry status. Freezing / Fire Grease for weakness targets.
  • Status pots & bone arrows — your zero-requirement "cast status from range": Rot/Poison/Sleep pots and Rotbone/Poisonbone/Sleepbone/Bloodbone arrows all proc full status with no stat needed. See Broken Builds.
  • Cure boluses — Stanching (bleed), Neutralizing (rot/poison), Thawfrost Boluses. Craft a full stack — a status proc on you at RL1 is lethal.
  • Pickled Turtle Neck & Immunizing Cured Meat — stamina regen and status resistance; keep both active for hard fights.

Spirit Ashes worth upgrading

  • Lhutel the Headless — early, tanky, teleports and throws spears. Superb aggro-sponge from the Weeping Peninsula catacombs.
  • Black Knife Tiche — late-game elite; high damage and a HP-cutting effect. Ringleader's Evergaol, Moonlight Altar (Liurnia).
  • Mimic Tear — copies your gear, so it applies your bleed/rot a second time. Weak health at RL1 but a great status-stacking distraction.
  • Upgrade all of them with Grave/Ghost Glovewort — hand Roderika your Chrysalids' Memento to unlock ash-tuning at the Roundtable Hold.
How strong you can actually get

Attribute Ceiling & Great Runes

Your eight stats are frozen at 10, but three level-independent sources raise them — and they stack additively. The ceiling tells you exactly which weapons you can wield without penalty and which spells you can cast at all (spells, unlike weapons, simply won't fire below their requirement). Every build below is bolted onto this.

The ceiling is 20 in every stat — Godrick's Great Rune (+5 all) on top of Radagon's Soreseal (+5 Vig/End/Str/Dex) and Marika's Soreseal (+5 Mnd/Int/Fai/Arc). Two-handing multiplies Strength by 1.5 on top of that, so a two-handed weapon sees up to Str 30 for meeting its requirement. That's what unlocks the heavier bleed weapons and the entire status-caster path — Faith/Arcane hit 15 with one Soreseal, 20 with the Great Rune stacked on. A single attribute can climb higher still if you commit your head slot to a stat helm (see below) — Arcane, for instance, can reach ~28.

The Sorseal tax. Each Soreseal increases damage taken by 15% (not less — the item wording reads like a benefit, but it's a penalty). Running both for the caster stats means +30% damage taken on a level-1 health bar, so reserve that config for ranged / caster play where you're not getting hit. For melee, one Soreseal (usually Radagon's) plus a survival talisman is the safer default.

Armor — a fourth stat source, but for requirements

A handful of armor pieces (almost all head-slot) raise an attribute, and they stack additively on top of the talismans and Great Rune, counting for both requirements and scaling. But at RL1 their real job is meeting a threshold, not adding damage: bleed buildup gains nothing until Arcane ~25 (a dead zone you can barely reach), and rot and frost buildup are fixed — they don't scale at all. So a stat helm earns its slot only when it lets you wield a heavy weapon at full damage (no under-req penalty) or hit a spell's cast requirement — the latter mandatory, since a spell won't fire below its stat. Each helm also costs you the White Mask (same head slot); only the DLC's Thiollier's Garb boosts a stat (Arc) from the chest.

Every broken toy in the box

Broken Builds

Raw damage never scales at RL1, so every build here wins one of three ways: status buildup (ignores your level entirely), a fixed-damage gimmick, or a summon doing the work. Two casting rules decide what's possible — (1) unlike weapons, a spell can't be cast at all unless you meet its Int/Faith requirement (there's no under-req penalty-cast), and (2) the only gear that raises casting stats is Marika's Soreseal (+5 Int/Faith/Arc/Mind, buried late in Elphael). So spell builds are largely an endgame luxury, while pots, bone arrows and weapon-status work from the very first hour.

Status builds — the win conditions

bleedHemorrhage stack

The staple. An innate-bleed weapon (Uchigatana, Rivers of Blood, Bloody Helice, Hookclaws, Bloodhound's Fang), Blood-infused, buffed by Seppuku + Lord of Blood's Exultation + the White Mask (Mohg's helm, +10% attack after a nearby bleed proc) + Shard of Alexander for skill damage. A bleed proc is ~15% of the target's max HP, so Rivers of Blood's Corpse Piler or a Seppuku'd katana drops most bosses in a handful of procs. Add Bloodflame Blade (incantation) to give bleed to any weapon that lacks it — though at Faith 12 it needs Marika's Soreseal to cast.

rotScarlet rot — melee poke

Antspur Rapier (innate rot) + Rot Grease + Kindred of Rot's Exultation (+20% attack near rot) + Rotten Winged Sword Insignia. Poke once or twice through your shield, back off, and rot devours a huge slice of the bar over its duration. Stacks with bleed for two health bars ticking at once.

any status · no statsStatus at range — pots & bone arrows

The RL1 "caster" that needs zero stats and works from hour one. Craft Rot / Poison / Sleep / Fire / Freezing pots and Rotbone / Poisonbone / Sleepbone / Bloodbone arrows (any low-Dex shortbow). Full status buildup, thrown from safety — lob a rot pot and a poison pot to stack two damage-over-time bars, or open with a sleep pot for a free critical. This is your always-available answer to "just cheese it from range."

rot / poisonCaster breath — rot & poison clouds endgame

The spell version, and it's a late unlock. Equip Marika's Soreseal (→ Faith/Arc 15) and cast Rotten Breath (Faith 15 / Arc 12) or Poison Mist (Faith 12) from any low-req seal — enormous status clouds that melt big bosses from range, buffed further by Kindred of Rot's Exultation. Note: Ekzykes's Decay (Faith 23) is not castable even with the Soreseal — Rotten Breath is the rot-breath you'll actually use. (Stacking Godrick's Great Rune's +5 pushes casting stats to 20, opening a few more spells, and at that 20-cap Swarm of Flies (Arcane 16) joins the kit as a castable bleed.) You can grab every piece early and bank it — the checklist picks up a Finger Seal, Poison Mist and Bloodflame Blade (which adds bleed to any weapon) long before you can cast them, so they're ready the instant Marika's Soreseal drops.

frostFrost — the multiplier

Hoarfrost Stomp, a Cold-infused weapon, Zamor Ice Storm, or the DLC's Divine Beast Frost Stomp. A frostbite proc bursts HP and raises the target's damage-taken by 20% — so it's less a standalone build than a force-multiplier bolted onto everything else: frost first, then bleed into the debuff.

sleepSleep — the free crit

Sword of St. Trina (its Mists of Slumber skill is an AoE sleep cloud; wielded under-requirement the sleep still procs at full) + Sleep pots + Sleepbone arrows + St. Trina's Torch. A slept boss takes a guaranteed charged critical and gives you a breather — deadly on the Godskins and most humanoids.

Buffs, nukes & carries

buffGreat Rune — a temporary second Soreseal

After a shardbearer falls, restore its Great Rune at the matching Divine Tower and activate it with a Rune Arc (buff lasts until you die). At RL1 two are gold: Godrick's Great Rune gives +5 to every attribute — extra HP/stamina and the requirement headroom to wield or even cast more (it stacks with Marika's Soreseal), and Morgott's Great Rune gives a large max-HP boost for survivability walls. Rune Arcs are limited, so bank them for boss attempts you're serious about.

one-boss nukeSerpent-Hunter (Rykard)

The great spear in Rykard's arena needs no stats and stun-locks him regardless of poise. Not a build so much as an "I win" button for that one fight — pick it up and spam the skill.

turtleFingerprint Shield + poke

Fingerprint Stone Shield (100% physical, elite stability even under-req) in the left hand + a thrusting weapon poked over the top. Purely physical bosses die without landing a hit; the Beast Crest Heater Shield is the early stand-in.

carrySpirit-ash carry

A maxed Mimic Tear copies your kit and applies your bleed/rot a second time; Black Knife Tiche chunks big health bars. Summon, break line of sight, and let them tank while status ticks. In the DLC, the Dried Bouquet talisman grants +20% attack for 30s after your summon dies.

scadutreeDLC — status on a rising floor

Great Katana / Bloodfiend's Arm (bleed) or Divine Beast Frost Stomp (frost), all riding the Scadutree Blessing — the only scaling in the Land of Shadow. Same status plan as the base game, now with a damage floor that climbs with every fragment you bank.

Techniques, not items

Universal Cheese Toolkit

These tricks work everywhere and rely only on the game's own AI, terrain and physics — no glitches. Learn them once and reuse them all run. Most bypass damage entirely, which is exactly what a Level 1 character needs.

Margit's Shackle — the Swiss-army cheese

You already buy it for the Margit fight (5,000 runes from Patches in Murkwater Cave). It does far more than stun a boss: the Shackle fires an invisible, no-damage AoE that "hits" everything in a wide radius, ignoring walls and obstructions. That one item does four jobs:

  • Stuns shackle-vulnerable bosses — Margit & Morgott (both forms) and Godrick: yanks them to the ground for a free critical, twice per phase.
  • Destroys catacomb Chariots — see the callout below; clears the deadliest hazard in the Hero's Grave dungeons for free.
  • Reveals illusory walls — the AoE registers as a hit on hidden walls, so you never have to guess-attack every surface.
  • Triggers pillar & fire traps from safety — pop trap mechanisms from across the room instead of standing in them.
chariotsHow the Shackle kills a Chariot

In the Hero's Grave dungeons (Auriza Hero's Grave in Altus, Gelmir Hero's Grave, etc.) the rolling spiked Chariots are summoned by sigils on the floor. Fire the Shackle at the long ramp holding two chariots — it registers as a hit that spawns the next chariot early, so they crash into and destroy each other. On the three-chariot puzzle it also raises the trap pillar for you from across the room. No dodging, no timing — and Auriza hands you the Tree Sentinel armor set + Ash of War: Holy Ground once all three are wrecked.

Gravity does your damage

Fall damage is lethal in a way that completely ignores your level, so walking a boss off a ledge is the ultimate RL1 equalizer. Enemy AI pathfinds badly around cliffs — especially while chasing you — so bait, back off, and let them misstep.

  • Great-Jar Duelists — get a knight stuck on the invisible arena wall, drop to a protruding lip of the cliff, throw a dagger/knife to keep its attention, and it shuffles forward off the edge. Repeat for all three. (Fails only if a knight rolls a ranged weapon like Mohg's Spear — reset for a melee set.)
  • Fire Giant — lure him to the tall rock on the arena's right edge, drop down with Torrent's double-jump, then coax him off. A fall instantly triggers his phase change (or kills him). See region 10.
  • Valiant Gargoyles — fight near the cliff edge; dodge into open air and the flying gargoyles frequently sail off to their deaths. See region 08.
  • Tree Sentinels & Runebears — genuinely dangerous on foot, trivial off a ledge; lure them onto uneven terrain where the AI stumbles over the drop.

Reusable habits

  • Summon & break line-of-sight — send in a Spirit Ash/NPC, then duck behind a pillar or wall. The boss commits to the summon while your bleed/rot ticks it down and you heal safely.
  • Torrent hit-and-run — any open arena (including the underground Ancestor Spirit / Regal Spirit fights, where Torrent is allowed) becomes a kiting game. Ride in, one combo, ride out.
  • Drop / plunging attacks — a jumping attack from a ledge deals big stagger and posture damage; several field bosses can be poked from above repeatedly.
  • Sleep-pot opener — on any sleep-vulnerable boss (Godskins, many humanoids), a thrown Sleep pot buys a guaranteed charged critical to open the fight.
  • Crouch to reset aggro — crouch-walking behind cover drops enemy awareness, letting you reposition, re-summon, or line up a backstab.
  • Disarm the copycats — the Mimic Tear boss in Nokron copies whatever you have equipped. Strip everything before the fog and it spawns in its underwear, punching for nothing (see region 08).
  • Parry items beat "unblockable" attacks — some special items deflect a boss's signature move regardless of your stats. The Blasphemous Claw parries Maliketh's Black Blade (see region 11).

Stealth & detection

  • Mimic's Veil — disguise yourself as an object to shed aggro, slip past a room, or reset a bad pull. Great for skipping fights you don't need.
  • Sentry's Torch — hold it (or two-hand your weapon to sling it on your back) to reveal invisible enemies — the fix for Ordina's Black Knife Assassins.
  • Concealing Veil / crouch-sneak — stay undetected to walk past packs, or line up a free opening backstab.

Stakes of Marika — free retries

Many hard bosses have a Stake of Marika planted right by their fog wall. Die to anything except fall damage and you can respawn there with a full flask — no run-back. Confirmed examples: outside Flying Dragon Agheel's lake and the opening Tree Sentinel (Limgrave), beside Decaying Ekzykes (Caelid), and at the Fire Giant's entrance (Mountaintops). Not every boss has one — night-only and NPC-triggered bosses often don't — but where a stake exists it turns a brutal fight into pure pattern practice with zero downtime.

Runes fund upgrades, not levels

Because you never level, every rune goes to Smithing Stones, greases, pots and flask stock. Two legit farms cover the whole run:

  • Greyoll (Caelid) — the sleeping dragon in Dragonbarrow. A single kill dumps ~14,000+ runes plus its dragonkin — an enormous early bankroll for buying out your first Smithing Stone tiers.
  • Mohgwyn Palace Albinaurics — from the Palace Approach Ledge-Road grace, sweep an AoE through the crawling Albinaurics on the slope, rest at the grace, repeat. Still one of the fastest farms in the game and completely legit (normal enemy respawns).
Get strong before you have to

Power Priority — be maxed for the moment

Your RL1 power is almost entirely level-independent, and most of it can be grabbed far earlier than the region it "belongs" to. So the checklist front-loads it: whenever a Soreseal, a Smithing Bell Bearing, a shield or a talisman becomes reachable, you detour for it before pushing the story. The goal is to meet every boss — even the first ones — already as strong as an RL1 character can possibly be at that point. See the Attribute Ceiling for the stat math that decides what you can wield and cast, and which Great Rune to slot.

Grab these in priority order

  • 1 · Radagon's Soreseal — Fort Faroth, no boss. ~26% more HP plus the Str/Dex to actually wield your kit. Get it almost first (Phase B).
  • 2 · Weapon upgrade level — your single biggest damage lever. Smithing Bell Bearing [1] is reachable before Godrick via the Stormhill→Liurnia skip; buy out the stones and keep every weapon at its tier cap. A +6 weapon at Stormveil hits like a much later one.
  • 3 · 100% shield + Bloodhound's Step — the Beast Crest Heater Shield (Stormhill, no boss) blocks all physical; Bloodhound's Step (Liurnia) is your i-frame dodge. Together that's near-total mitigation from the first legacy dungeon onward.
  • 4 · Mimic Tear — the moment the Radahn festival is available (reach Altus, or progress Ranni's quest), rush Radahn → Nokron for it. It doubles your status output and tanks — the biggest mid-game spike.
  • 5 · Exultation talismans + Seppuku — Lord of Blood's / Kindred of Rot's each add +20% attack on a proc; Seppuku's self-bleed multiplies hemorrhage. Flat multipliers on everything you already do.
  • 6 · Maxed Spirit Ashes + flasks — feed Glovewort to your Mimic/Tiche and sweep every Golden Seed & Sacred Tear. Pure free scaling.
  • 7 · DLC — Scadutree Blessing + Revered Ashes — the only scaling in the Land of Shadow; top it up before every fight.
